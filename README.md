# Corporate Credit Risk Analysis: Vedanta Limited (NSE: VEDL)

An institutional-grade risk assessment scorecard and credit memorandum evaluating the financial and operational risk profile of **Vedanta Limited** (Metals & Mining / Diversified Natural Resources).

> **Disclaimer:** These models and reports were created for academic and professional portfolio development purposes. The analysis relies on historical financial disclosures and assumptions constructed solely to demonstrate financial modeling proficiency. They do not constitute formal investment, legal, or financial advice.

## 📌 Project Overview
This model acts as a commercial banking or rating agency scorecard. By weighing core balance sheet ratios against rigorous thresholds, it assigns an internal credit rating and provides an outlook on the company's debt servicing capabilities.

## 🛠️ Key Metrics Tracked & Financial Frameworks
* **Liquidity & Leverage Scorecard:** Analyzed key risk ratios against institutional safety thresholds:
  * *Net Debt/EBITDA:* Stood at a strong 1.5x (Well within the safe threshold of ≤ 3.0x).
  * *Interest Coverage Ratio (ICR):* 3.2x EBITDA/Interest (Comfortably above the ≥ 2.5x buffer threshold).
  * *Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR):* Flagged at **0.7x** (Below the crucial ≥ 1.1x threshold).
  * *Current Ratio:* Flagged at **0.7x** (Below the working capital baseline of ≥ 1.2x).
* **Bankruptcy Risk Modeling:** Implemented the **Altman Z-Score** framework, calculating a score of **2.12** to gauge structural corporate distress.
* **Qualitative Risk Overlays:** Factored in cash flow tracking (FCF), capital expenditure pressure, dividend payout frequencies, and royalty/dividend leakage to the parent entity, Vedanta Resources.

## 📋 Internal Credit Rating Output
* **Final Weighted Score:** **3.00 / 5.00** (where 1.0 is best, 5.0 is worst).
* **Implied Internal Credit Rating:** **BBB** (Investment Grade Baseline).
* **Rating Outlook:** **Negative**.
* **Credit Opinion:** Vedanta's robust core EBITDA generation (₹38,601 Cr) is structurally offset by an aggressive working capital mismatch (sub-1.0x current and debt service ratios) and continuous cash drainage to its parent entity.
